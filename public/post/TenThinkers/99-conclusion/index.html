A New Age of Discovery

There once was an age of discovery. Names like Christopher Columbus, Magellan, For two hundred years, the Western kingdoms roamed around on ships in search of new lands to colonize or deplete of riches. Literally, the world-view of the West changed rapidly, and history was forever altered.

Now there is a sense that there is nothing left for man to discover on this planet1. Our lands are discovered. With the digital tools like Google Maps, navigation is finding the most efficient routes to wherever you want to go. No need for a paper map or even memory. The question is no longer “Is anything out there?”, or even “What is out there?”, but instead “What do I want?”, “Where do I want to go?”. The mystery of what lies beyond has turned to new frontiers: space, human consciousness, and the ocean. Making a discovery in these fields is now largely only possible if one is a specialized scientist with funding, and that is not most of us. There is a sense that we are finished with the Earth. It is a closed book, and we’re ready for something new and exciting, like Mars.

In a materialistic world, perhaps this is true, that discovery of new lands, charting uncharted territory is, for the meanwhile, taken care of.
