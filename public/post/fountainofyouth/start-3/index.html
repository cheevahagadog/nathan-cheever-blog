By Nathan Cheever

Tess: Great to see you Jordan. Tell me, how was New Life +? Jordan: Hey Tess. It was fantastic. I am more committed than ever… After all, anything else than this is stupid and suicidal. Some people believe mortality is still meaningful. I pity them. Tess: What about Grandpa? Jordan: Grandpa is on his way out. He’s just too old-fashioned to consider anything other than the status quo. Tess: What about Mike then? You know, while you were gone he had quite the setback. Jordan: What’s a setback when you have all the time in the world? It’s his fault, but I don’t pity him. He’s young enough to know better.

(Someone): Why even do it? Why grow old? What does it accomplish – letting your body go to waste, waiting to lose your basic functions and sanity? Yeah sure you might have more memories, more lessons learned, but your future– you have no future! No chance to apply that wisdom to lead a better life. What if you could gain that wisdom and knowledge over a typical human life and then continue on?

(Someone): Aging cannot be said to be good because it is natural. There are a lot of natural things that I’d like to do without. Mosquitos, lice, disease. I for one would gladly live in a world without those.
