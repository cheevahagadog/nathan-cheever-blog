Chapter 1

In the year of 2025, mankind was redeemed. In 2025 the human race was finally and truly saved.

For some reason, that year ushered in a downpour of scientific advancement and serendipity, that people’s chronic In the year 2025, three different scientists came out with incredible discoveries. The first was a team from MIT, lead by Dr. F. After years of study, they were able to isolate and replicate a bacteria capable of eating and breaking down plastic waste. This bacteria specimen thrived on eating this plastic food, and showed significant results. A culture of this stuff could decompose a plastic water bottle in less than a week. An astonishing achievement! And their lab had successfully created a sludge mix that could be spread aerially over waste sites. The byproduct of the decomposition process was oxygen. This was hailed as the first miracle.

The second of these miracles was announced when a collaborative effort of scientists in the UK and Berkeley created a drug that enabled cells to reproduce without any errors. One’s DNA would be copied without a mistake. Tests showed conclusively that this treatment had halted and reversed aging in clinical trials. Their treatment, taken once by a pill, effectively, and so far conclusively, solved the problem of decay, error, and breakdown in animal cells, also known as aging. With one treatment of this drug, taken orally, aging would be gone. A youth who took it would continue to develop until their prime, as governed by natural processes. But once the age of full physical maturity had been reached, life would go on in that way.

These veritable miracles of science landed the leading scientists on the cover of every magazine. All three received a Nobel prize. These scientists became instant celebrities. It was clear that these discoveries were going to change the world forever. Dr F., at her acceptance speech that went viral, proclaimed the salvation of the human race from the terrors of physical death. One part that was replayed often was this:

“Science and human ingenuity has finally allowed us to step beyond the limits of nature and into immortality. Through technology, we have overcome our human weakness. Age, disease, suffering are no longer inevitable. Human suffering can be a thing of the past. Until today, time was the only thing one couldn’t get more of. Today, all that changes. Science has remade the world, saved the environment, and given mankind a new life, a new hope, and a new and bright future.”

The reception was immense. People were happier. It was clear that these discoveries were different than any other. Their arrival marked a turning point for humanity, like the discovery of fire and tools. For many it was unsettling to know the world was going to change. And amidst all the general optimism and excitement, many were wondering just what the effect would be.
